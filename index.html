Introduction

ILITHA is rolling out fibre optic internet in Ward 14, Mdantsane. This project uses FTTH (Fibre-to-the-Home) technology, ensuring fast, stable, and future-proof internet. This manual is designed for both sales teams and technicians to understand, sell, and maintain the network effectively.

How the Network Works

Think of fibre like a road system:

  • Main Feeder Cable (Highway): Carries lots of data long distances.
  • Distribution Routes (Main Roads): Branch into neighborhoods.
  • Poles (Streetlights): Carry fibre overhead closer to homes.
  • Splitters (Intersections): Divide fibre into smaller lines (1 cable â†’ 8 homes).
  • Joints (Connection Boxes): Where cables are spliced and linked.
  • DPs (Distribution Points): Final pole connection before fibre goes into homes.

From the DP, fibre runs into the house, into an ONT, and connects to Wi-Fi routers.

Roles of Teams

Sales Teams

Confirm coverage, explain fibre is in the street, and sell packages.

Technicians

Check nearest pole, monitor splitter capacity, maintain joints, and report issues.

Troubleshooting

  • No Connection: Check DP on nearest pole â†’ trace to Joint.
  • Slow Speeds: Check splitter load (1:8 rule).
  • Broken Cable: Replace aerial fibre between poles.
  • Power Issue: Ensure ONT at customer premises has power.
